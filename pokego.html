Pokémon Go Onboarding

Pokémon Go is perhaps the most widely played augmented reality game ever. In it, players catch imaginary creatures called Pokémon, level them up, and battle for territory.

When I first played Pokémon Go it struck me how poorly written the introductory tutorial and help text was. So, as an independent project, I decided to rewrite and redesign the Pokémon Go onboarding process.

Process

Project Justifications

This project is an unsolicited redesign. Clear justifications are needed.

  • The original tutorial is poorly organized: Information doesn't scope from broad to general well. The tutorial often teaches specifics before teaching general ideas.
  • Language is bad: Dialogue is often stilted and unnatural, and ideas are communicated over more screens than necessary.
  • There are large gaps in its content: Basic game functions, like throwing Poké Balls are not mentioned at all in the tutorial.

Design Process

  1. Conduct a content audit and critique: The original totals 56 screens over 6 distinct sections. I conducted a thorough critique of the original content for overall coherence, clarity and economy of language/screens.
  2. Create an information hierarchy of needs: Identify what information is currently conveyed, what information is missing, and when it should be presented to the user.
  3. Rework the narrative flow so that content moves from broad topics (context, world building) to narrow ones (Item use).
  4. Identify key ideas in each section, and design these screens so they can be accessed at any time by the user like an in-game manual.
  5. Rewrite existing dialogue when necessary in natural-sounding spoken English, with an eye towards reducing screen count.
  6. Respect the joy of discovery: The original writers didn't want to give away too much: things were left vague. Therefore, help text should be helpful and clear but non-leading, allowing the user to discover things for themselves.

Outcome

The redesign consists of 48 screens in the main tutorial. It also includes contextual help screens, which are displayed independent of the main tutorial, as well as Tips screens which are accessible only through the Tips menu.
